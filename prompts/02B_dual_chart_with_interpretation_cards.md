LAYOUT_NAME: dual_chart_with_interpretation_cards
WHEN_TO_USE: When the slide needs two complementary charts showing different quantitative angles on the same topic, plus structured interpretation below. Pick this when (a) research provides data for two distinct chart types (e.g., scatter + bar, trend line + ranking), (b) the charts together reveal a pattern that neither shows alone, and (c) the audience needs explicit "so what" cards connecting evidence to action.
DATA_REQUIRES: market_data

TITLE: "[Insight-driven title connecting both chart perspectives - e.g., 'High-loyalty brands grow 2.1x faster, but only 3 of 12 players sit in the target zone']"

LAYOUT: Two side-by-side charts on top, three numbered interpretation cards on bottom.

TOP ZONE - DUAL CHARTS:
- Left chart panel: chart with analytical sub-header framing the question (e.g., "Market share vs. customer retention"). Best for scatter, line, or area charts showing relationships or trends.
- Below chart: 1-2 sentence interpretation explaining the primary insight and its strategic implication.
- Right chart panel: chart with analytical sub-header framing the comparison (e.g., "YoY revenue growth by brand"). Best for bar or column charts showing rankings or breakdowns.
- Both charts share consistent axis labeling and source attribution.

BOTTOM ZONE - THREE INTERPRETATION CARDS:
- Three equal-width cards, each with a numbered circle badge and bold judgment title.
- Card 1 - Dominant pattern: what jumps out when both charts are read together. Body references specific data points from both charts.
- Card 2 - Structural mechanism: why the pattern exists (causation, not correlation). Body connects drivers across the two views.
- Card 3 - Risk or opportunity: what to do about it (action-oriented). Body names the strategic implication.
- Each card ends with a bottom metric tag: one standout number reinforcing the card argument (e.g., "-> 2.1x retention multiplier").

BOTTOM: "Key takeaway: [cross-element synthesis linking the dominant pattern to the recommended strategic response]"

Source: [Real report names with year, semicolon-separated]
